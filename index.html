goZero

Solo Project

goZero is a resource guide for living a “zero-waste” lifestyle or for those trying to reduce the amount of daily waste they produce. When users navigate to the goZero home page they see a full-page map with markers indicating the location of businesses that support zero-waste practices, such as package-free bulk shopping. A window positioned on top of the map contains a list of all the businesses marked on the map. Users can click on each listing to get descriptive information and see their zero-waste offerings. Registered users have the capability to add new businesses to the list, update those they have added, and delete them.

Technologies used: MongoDb, Express, AngularJS, Node.js, Google Maps API, AngularJS Material, Passport, JavaScript, HTML5

Oasis for Youth

Group Project

As part of a 5-person development team I helped build a full-stack application for Oasis, a non-profit organization that runs a drop-in resource center for at-risk youth. Our web application streamlines and organizes the volunteer registration process so that staff can spend their time working on other important program areas. The application will replace a paper-based registration process with an online submission form. The application provides the client with a filterable quick-view page of all volunteers, their stage in the registration process, their areas of interest, length of service, and hours worked. I built the front-end logic for the filterable quick-view page and served as the point of contact between the client and our team.

Technologies used: postgreSQL, Express, AngularJS, Node.js, AngularJS Material, Passport, JavaScript, HTML5, Nodemailer
